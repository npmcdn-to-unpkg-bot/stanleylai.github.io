InkCartridges.com is a brand of LD Products, a leading online retailer of printer supplies in the US.

Seeking new opportunities for growth, they identified building a mobile storefront as a strategic necessity. My team at Mobify collaborated with their Marketing team to develop this new mobile experience.

My Role

I was the lead Interaction Designer on the project, leading the design process as well as writing the production front-end UI markup.

We were a small team of Designers, Project Managers, Front-end Engineers and QA Analysts working on a tight deadline.

Key Challenges

Understanding our Audience

Shoppers on InkCartridges.com behave differently compared to your typical ecommerce site. Picking up a new order of ink is typically a utilitarian desire, and one you want to get done quickly and right away. We saw this is the core insight motivating our design.

Diverse User Backgrounds

Our audience run the gamut from seasoned professionals such as office managers looking to stock their office, to the non-savvy family that needs consumables for the home printer.

First Steps

Understanding the Customer

As one of the larger online retailers for printer consumables, we tapped their expertise and knowledge to understand their customers. We combined this with our own guerilla tests and heuristics to find the way forward.

Hierarchical Task Analysis

We studied the key interactions and tasks that customers needed to perform in order to purchase the consumables they wanted. Organizing and sorting them, we identified the key steps and optimized our mobile experience around them.

Design Focus

Product Search Experience

We pared down the experience to the essentials, and streamlined the steps to finding their desired product. We targeted specific points of friction, and improved them â€“ such as a quick filter to shorten product lists.

Outcome

We managed to signicantly reduce the complexity and time it took for the average customer to complete a purchase on mobile vs desktop. This led to great satisfaction from the client team, and considerations on expanding this to their other brands.
