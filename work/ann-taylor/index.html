--- layout: work title: Ann Taylor & LOFT ---

Ann Taylor is a speciality apparel retail chain with clothing targeted at the affluent career woman.

Founded in 1954, they have since developed additional brands such as LOFT and Lou & Grey with unique styles targeting a new and younger demographic.

Our team at Mobify worked with Ann Taylor's UX and Brand teams to leveraging our tech to deliver a mobile storefront that enabled them to better engage an increasingly sophisticated audience.

My Role

I was the lead Interaction Designer on the project, leading the design process that defined the interactions and visual design.

We were a small team of Designers, Project Managers, Front-end Engineers, UI Developers and QA Analysts working on a tight deadline, and I dived into the code often to give the UI developers a hand.

Challenge

Defining the Digital Identity

The Ann Taylor Brand team had done an amazing job developing standards for their print and in-store retail environments. We worked with them to define their digital brand standards.

Understanding their Users

The client team has done extensive customer research, interviews, and testing to determine the priorities, behaviour patterns, and preferences of their target audience. We learnt and distilled their knowledge and applied it the design of their mobile experience.

High Stakes

Executives at Ann Taylor placed a high priority on delivering an excellent mobile experience to allow them to compete strongly in the upcoming shopping season.

First Steps

Client Co-Design

The Ann Taylor team of stakeholders involved in the project was very large and ran the gamut from executives and managers to designers and researchers.

We used an intimate co-design process to help align the various stakeholders, collaborating on content auditing, wire-framing etc.

Heuristic Analysis

Together with the client team, we conducted a heuristic study of their current desktop site and exploratory prototypes they had developed internally.

Alongside exposing potential areas for improvement, we acquainted oursleves with their motivations behind particular design approaches. This provided us with additional insight towards our design process.

Design Focus

Style Tiles

We used Style Tiles as an iterative approach to developing their larger visual identity. Starting with the bascis – e.g. type treatments and colours, we eventually worked towards the widgets and components that would make the complete UI.

Product Listing page

We kept the UI lean, with the visual focus on the items listed. The features we modified and created were specifically chosen based on our mutual research into the key priorities and preferences within their customers’ decision-making process.

Product Description Page

We did quick tests on Ann Taylor's target demographic, and determined that apparel options such as available colours and sizes were paramount. Rather than hide them in dropdown boxes, we exposed them in large option panels for greater accessibility efficiency.

Checkout Flow

We studied the checkout flow and identified key areas where processes could be simplified and complexities hidden. There were technical challenges with AJAX transformations on mobile, and we designed around them by hiding certain portions behind modals.

Outcome

The launch was a resounding success. Customers experienced greater satisfaction and found mobile their preferred way to shop. Mobile conversion rates exceeded that of desktop, helping Ann Taylor reach ROI within just 2 weeks.
